Stanford "A," early 1670s possibly 1669

Stanford M132/2/4 ALCH00100
    This MS is the "De scriptoribus chemicis" (DSC). It is a list of 84 book titles, "written in a single operation," with an "anomalous addition" of 'Et Lond. 1692.8', to an entry for 'Peusti' on f.1v, and 6 appended entries on f.3r set off by a short horizontal rule. The DSC contains bibliographic information useful to book-sellers. § FHP p.136 § f.1v § f.3r
    Dobbs thought this MS belonged to "Newton's late period." FHP estimate the early 1670s. Whiteside suggested this MS was written in 'the late 1660s even before April 1669' when Newton purchased Zetzner's 6-volume Theatrum Chemicum, based on handwriting. § FHP p.137
    The DSC was copied directly from Pierre Borel's Bibliotheca Chimica (Paris, 1654). § FHP p.138
    The DCS is full of desiderata, usually headed 'Desiderantur'. It was aspirational—most of the books listed were in his library at the end. § FHP p.144
    There was no mention of Sendivogius or Maier in the DSC, even though they were important sources for young Newton. § FHP p.144
    Note: for timelining, we interpret 'early 1670s' as Jan 1670-Dec 1673.

"Lib. Chem." 1670-75

Babson MS 419 ALCH00092
    This MS, the 'English counterpart' of DSC, is "a comprehensive listing of 120 authors' names . . . in alphabetical order . . . summarizing . . . lives and/or . . . works" based on Maier's Symbola Aureae Mensae Duodecim Nationum. The MS is "dated to 1670-75 by handwriting." § FHP p.142, n.22.

"Keynes 13 A/B/C," part A late 1680s[??], part B early 1690s, part C 1700s

Keynes MS 13 ALCH00002
    This MS consists of "a series of classifying listings which again in essence were based on Maier," Symbola Aureae Mensae Duodecim Nationum. § FHP p.142, n.25.
    Keynes 13 sheet 3 f.1v has a 'Desiderantur' passage written upside down to the rest of the text on the sheet. § FHP p.145. § Keynes MS 13 sheet 3.
    FHP say "Part B contains a Borel 'Desiderantur,'" so Part B must be our sheet 3, and that could be from the early 1690s as they say. It bears the dates '1684' and '1689', and the latter date could reasonably be taken as a likely terminus post quem for sheet 3.
    It's not clear from FHP, I think, which sheet, 1 or 2, is their Part A and which is their Part C, but both sheets bear Mint business, so "late 1680s" for either of those seems unlikely and Newton's arrival at the Mint in spring 1696 is likely a minimum post-quem date for both sheets 1 and 2, their A and C. Sheet 3, their B, could qualify as (very very) "late 1680s" if written in 1689, but not 1 and 2. Are FHP confused in their identification of B?
